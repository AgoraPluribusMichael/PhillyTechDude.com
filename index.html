Innovative Ideas and Policy Solutions from a Citizen Who Cares

Are you frustrated by a political system that seems stuck in gridlock, dominated by corporate lobbyists, and disconnected from the everyday needs of ordinary people?

In this section, I outline existing procedures and underutilized tools — like the discharge petition — that ordinary people could utilize to fight to make the government work for us — not themselves, the corporate special interests, or their donors.

Economy

Our profit-driven corporate economy too often rewards large corporations for destroying jobs and degrading the quality and quantity of their products and services. Let's flip the incentives. In this section, I argue that we should invest in small business owners instead of large corporations so that we can restore the economy as an engine for American prosperty.

Taxes

In this section, I make the case for why a wealth gains tax can help us force the wealthy to pay their fair share in taxes so that the government can continue to fund invaluable programs that serve the American public.

About Me

I'm a 28-year-old software engineer living in South Philly who is considering getting into public policy or advocacy after 6 years in the software engineering industry, because I'm tired of writing code designed to make the rich people richer, and I would instead prefer to advocate for and write laws to help serve the common good.

I never formally studied law, public policy, or political science; I'm just a guy who lives here, and I'm fed up waiting for the next big establishment Democratic politician to save us from Trump while the whole world devolves into madness.

All opinions and arguments that appear on this site are my own. Additionally, except where expressly denoted, all policies that appear on this site are ones that I've engineered myself, not as an expert, but as a layman and freedom-minded tech dude who came up with my own solutions and formed my own opinions through my own observations, my own research, and my own critical thought.
