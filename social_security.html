Let's Provide Health Savings Accounts for All Children

And Invest in Small Businesses at the Same Time

The first proposal is to provide Health Savings Accounts (HSAs) for all children (stretch goal: also adults under 26), allow parents and legal guardians to make tax-deferred contributions to the account, seed the account at birth, and invest the funds in a US-Treasury-managed high-yield small cap bond fund (with a strong value factor tilt), running at a negative expense ratio. Let's Protect, Expand, and Modernize Social Security with Basic Income

A Plan-B Program for When Social Security Runs Out of Money

The second proposal is to implement a new Basic Income program that initially mimics the structure of Social Security in every way but name. Furthermore, we can set up a Basic Income Trust Fund and make the dispersal of funds contingent on the total depletion of the Social Security Trust Fund.

If we set up this kind of Basic Income program, it is okay for the Social Security Trust Fund to run out of money. I would even argue that Basic Income is better than Social Security, since the idea of using a wealth tax to fund tax relief has appeal outside the duopoly and opens up the opportunity for an expansion of Basic Income in the future.

And as an American of Japanese descent, who has a living relative that spent some of her childhood in one of FDR's internment camps, I will say that if we can create a new Basic Income program that mimics Social Security, then I personally wouldn't be too upset if we allowed the Social Security Trust Fund to deplete, because we'll then have a modern Plan-B program that's waiting in the wings, ready to pick up the pieces on day one.

It will always be difficult to change Social Security, so there's a possibility it will stick around for a while. (Also, what's to stop the program from running at a deficit, like the entire government has for basically my whole life?) But if we can create a Basic Income system that's funded through wealth taxes or corporate taxes instead of payroll taxes, then there may be a reason to gradually repeal and replace Social Security with a Basic Income system designed for the 21st Century, without any interruption to the vital service for our seniors,
